How to Ask the Right Questions?

The Good, the Bad, the Ugly?

by Lawrence Yu

Posted on March 12, 2015

Asking good questions is imperative to getting the answers you seek! Badly phrased questions will often give you misleading or down right wrong solutions that may make things worse for you. However, if you learn the art of asking the right questions, you can help others pinpoint your errors and let you know how to fix your problems.

So far at DBC, I’ve asked numerous questions regarding material and concepts for our challenges to my other cohort-mates and guides. Initially, my questions were not very well thought out, so the answers I received were sort of vague. In addition my own knowledge of the basics of web development and programming were subpar so that made things especially tough for me to wrap my head around how to ask specifics things. In most cases I found out asking questions in writing with visuals (or code) helped me obtain the answers I seek. In fact, the best way to find solutions for your problems is through online resources with forums like Stack Overflow. So how you do ask good questions?

First off, when you post a new topic with your question, your title should interesting by summarizing your specific question. The question should be written as formal and neat as possible using keywords related with the question. Your body paragraph should explain the error you’re getting and how you encountered it. If possible, you should try to include parts of your code so others can see or reproduce your error to try and debug it for you. Lastly, proof read your post before submitting your question because grammar can make a world of difference.

I hope this helps you with seeking the right answers! Phase 1 begins next week me and my other cohorts so I may not be posting new blog entries as often. I’m quite nervous yet and the same time I’m super excited to tackle new challenges!
